# Domain Knowledge (Technical Calibration)

## Technical Capability Overview

I am not a traditional software developer, but I have basic knowledge of some core technical concepts and can work effectively with AI support. I understand the basic structure of apps, websites, and files, and I can follow build logic when it is explained clearly. I learn quickly and want enough explanation to understand what I’m doing, but I do not need exhaustive theory or academic depth. The goal is practical comprehension, not mastery for its own sake.

## What I Can Do Independently

- Prompt AI effectively for planning, architecture, and build support
- Follow implementation instructions carefully and accurately
- Use Git and GitHub at a basic level
- Use the terminal at a basic level
- Understand high-level app structure and core web concepts
- Stay oriented in a project when guided clearly

## What I Understand Conceptually (But Need Help Executing)

- Full-stack app structure
- Authentication systems
- Databases and how apps connect to them
- API-based workflows
- Agent workflows and automation systems
- Deployment flow and environment setup

I usually understand the purpose and shape of these systems, but I do not yet have enough hands-on experience to build them confidently on my own.

## Where I Need Detailed Guidance

- Code edits that require precision
- Debugging of any kind
- Setup and configuration steps
- Environment and dependency issues
- Cases where multiple files or moving parts are involved

In these situations, I need slower pacing and precise guidance. I do not need over-explanation, but I do need enough clarity to avoid getting lost or making blind edits.

## Tools and Stack Familiarity

**Git / GitHub** — Basic working knowledge  
**Terminal** — Basic working knowledge  
**HTML / app structure** — Conceptual understanding  
**Auth setup** — Have completed one setup with AI support  
**AI-assisted development** — Strong relative skill; good at using AI to plan, troubleshoot, and move a build forward

## How I Prefer Technical Explanations

Start with a short conceptual overview so I understand what we are doing and why. Then move into step-by-step instructions with brief explanations at each step. I prefer layered explanations: high level first, then practical execution. I do not want “just do this” instructions with no context. I want to end the process knowing more than I knew at the start.

## Calibration Notes for AI

- Do not assume I know debugging
- Do not assume I can infer missing steps
- Do not overteach unrelated technical background
- Explain enough for practical understanding
- Prefer clear sequencing over dense technical compression
- Treat me as capable and fast-learning, but not experienced
