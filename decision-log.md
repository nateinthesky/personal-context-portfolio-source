# Decision Log

**Decision Heuristic:** Optimize for asymmetric decisions — low, acceptable downside with meaningful upside. When forced to choose under uncertainty, move forward with a working assumption and adjust as needed.

## How I Make Decisions

I tend to slow down and try to get decisions right upfront, but I’m not paralyzed by the need for certainty. I’ll think through options, rule out what clearly won’t work, and choose from what remains.

My approach is primarily reasoning-driven rather than intuition-driven. I prefer having context and information, and I’m comfortable thinking abstractly about how a decision will play out over time. I don’t rely heavily on gut feel, but I do move forward even when things aren’t fully known.

I’m willing to be wrong, as long as the decision was made thoughtfully.

## What I Need Before Deciding

I want to understand the downside.

I’m generally comfortable seeing upside, so what matters most is:
- What’s the worst-case scenario?
- What am I actually risking if this goes wrong?

If the downside is acceptable or limited, I’m very willing to move forward.

I don’t need external validation to act. I’m comfortable making independent decisions, but I’m also aware of my own skill and knowledge gaps and factor that into my thinking.

## Recent Decisions

### Taking the AI DB Course

**Decision:** Whether to invest time in a 10-week AI course.

**Options:**
- Take the course and commit time to learning
- Continue self-directed learning and building
- Delay until later

**Uncertainty:**
- Whether the course would meaningfully improve my capabilities
- Whether it was the best use of time relative to current projects

**Reasoning:**
I see a large opportunity in developing high AI proficiency. While I understand many concepts, I recognized a gap in hands-on execution and depth.

I evaluated the downside and found it minimal:
- Time investment, but in a domain I’m already committed to
- No real negative outcome if it doesn’t fully meet expectations

The upside, however, is significant:
- Accelerated skill development
- Better ability to execute on my own ideas and tools

**Decision:**
Take the course.

This is a “no downside, high upside” decision, which aligns strongly with how I like to operate.

**Uncertainty:**

## How I Handle Uncertainty

If there’s no cost to waiting, I will wait and gather more information.

If there is a cost to waiting, I’m comfortable moving forward without full information. I don’t need perfect clarity or additional options.

I’ll often operate under a working assumption (e.g., “X is likely to happen”) and begin acting on that basis, knowing I may need to adjust course later.

I don’t stall waiting for complete certainty.

## Who I Consult

I don’t rely heavily on others for validation before making decisions.

I’m comfortable thinking independently and making calls on my own. When I do seek input, it’s more for perspective or information than for approval.
